About Us

Axios the technical club of The Indian Institute of Information Technology, Lucknow was constituted in 2019 with the aim to spread applied computer science culture among the students of our institute. It is a community of highly enthusiastic students doing coding, ethical hacking, Web Designing and all other aspects of programming.

What we do?

We create a platform that allows students to gain assistance and mentorship to enhance their coding ability. We believe that every student here at IIITL should have an opportunity to learn how to code and develop a passion for it. The secret of getting ahead is getting started and we aim to provide every student with the right start.

We conduct sessions and workshops, hold lectures and talks, and even host coding competitions and hackathons. There is something for everyone. Whether you want to learn, participate in hackathons and win big prizes, or prepare for the ACM-ICPC and GSoC, you can do it all here. We have a list of events planned up for the coming year.

Keep watching this space to know more.

Batch 2019-2020

The 2019-2020 batch of Axios also referred to as the “Founding Batch”, consisted of students committed to propagating the enthusiasm for coding in the institute and especially amongst freshmen.
From day zero, every member took steps daily to make sure that every student benefits from it, in the year 2019-20 the technical club brought many laurels and fame to the college, the club produced 3 GSoC selections, 3 teams qualified for ACM ICPC regionals and one made it till the West Asia Finals with a 9th rank in the regionals, the students even went on to win many hackathons, CTFs and other technical events.

Competitive Coding

Competitive Programming is a sport, we mean literally. Take any sport, let’s consider cricket for that matter, you walk in to bat for the first time. Swing and a miss, do it couple of times and you’ll eventually hit one over the ropes. Now, consider a programming contest as a game of cricket, metaphorically. Compile a code and submit, you may get a WA (Wrong Answer). Make changes to code and eventually you will get your first AC (Accepted/Correct Answer).

Let us give you a sneak peek, about 20% of questions in a programming contest are simple conversion of plain english to a code of your favourite programming language.
